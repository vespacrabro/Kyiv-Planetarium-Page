History: A Very Short Introduction

Planetarium today

The Kiev Planetarium opened in January 1952. Today it is a special combination of a specially designed building and a synthesized system consisting of an optical-mechanical device "Planetarium" and a digital projection system that allows you to display on the screen as a hemisphere images any information about the Earth and the Universe with the visual perception of knowledge in various fields of science.

In 1987, a new building of the Kyiv Planetarium was completed and opened at 57/3 Velyka Vasylkivska Street. Klim Ivanovich Churyumov - a prominent Ukrainian astronomer, corresponding member of the National Academy of Sciences of Ukraine, became the director of the planetarium.

The Kyiv Planetarium has a large lecture hall with a hemispherical dome 23 meters in diameter and 11.5 meters high, which is the second largest in Europe, and the building itself has a special architectural style with open space, and a unique architectural feature - a spiral ramp, symbolizing constant development and progress.
